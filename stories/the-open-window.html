The Open Window

খোলা জানালা

For nine months the window stayed open. From his bed he could see a square of sky, and the sky was never still. In the mornings it was a calm and ordinary blue. By afternoon the dark clouds gathered and played above the rooftops, and the thunder came with its low rumble, and the lightning, and then the rain falling in long bright threads. He watched all of it. Sleep had left him, and so the nights were long, but the open window gave him the moon and the slow gathering of stars, and that was something to hold.

He was not afraid. He told himself this, and he found that it was true. He had carried confidence all his life and it had not left him now. What filled the sleepless hours instead was memory, the golden days arriving one after another. He remembered the household of his parents, warm and whole. He remembered his mother's hand, and the worn copy of Thakurmar Jhuli she would open, and her voice carrying him into those old tales until the room around him fell away.

Parvin stayed close. He would watch her move quietly through the long days and find himself amazed by her, by how steadily she carried the weight of it all. The two boys were not in the house. They were abroad, on the far side of the world, and so they reached him through the small lit screen of the phone. Their voices were anxious. Abbu, one of them said, you promised us. You said you would not leave us.

And then they would speak of the car. They reminded him of the drives, of the road opening ahead and Ammu in the seat beside him, and the old song they always played, ও সোনা ব্যাঙ, filling the small space with its tune. Do not be afraid, they told him. We are coming, the two of us together. You will drive again. Ammu will sit beside you. The song will play, just as before.

He did not break. The confidence that had always been his was his still. The months passed, and the body that had held him to that bed slowly gave him back to himself. Two years after the window and the rain, he sat once more behind the wheel and drove onto the old campus, the grounds he knew so well. The day was bright. He was happy in a way that is hard to put into words. Somewhere the song was playing, or perhaps by now he simply carried it within him, ও সোনা ব্যাঙ, and the road ran on ahead.
